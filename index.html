Answer the following questions:

  1. What is the main source of water bodies?

    The main source of water bodies is rain water.

  2. How do we store river water and rain water?

    • Rain water is stored by building tanks.
    • River water is stored in reservoirs by constructing dams.

Benefits of Dams

  1. What is a Dam?

    A dam is defined as a barrier constructed to hold the backwaters and to raise its level by forming a reservoir. Dams are used to generate electricity and as a water resource.

  2. Where are dams constructed?

    Dams are usually constructed across the rivers to store water. They are built for a variety of purposes such as drinking, irrigation, and power generation.

  3. What rivers flow through our state?

    The major rivers in the state are Krishna and Godavari. Other rivers listed are Vamsadhara, Nagavali and Pennar.

  4. Name the reservoirs in our state?

    Major projects built across the rivers include the Nagarjunasagar and Srisailam dams, which are multipurpose dams built across the river Krishna. The Sir Arthur Thomas Cotton barrage is built across the river Godavari at Dowleswaram. The balancing reservoirs mentioned are Pulichintala, Teluguganga, Thotapalli barrage, Kandaleru reservoir, and Veligonda, which are mainly intended for agriculture and drinking purposes.

Answer the following questions:

  1. Which is the fourth largest river in India?

    The river Krishna is the fourth largest river in India.

  2. Where does the Krishna river's journey start?

    The river Krishna starts its journey from Mahabaleswar in Maharashtra.

  3. How long is the Krishna river?

    The river is almost 1400 kilometers long.

  4. Name the states through which river Krishna flows?

    Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, and Andhra Pradesh.

  5. In which districts does Krishna river flow in our state?

    Kurnool, Nandyal, Palnadu, Guntur, Bapatla, N.T.R., and Krishna.
